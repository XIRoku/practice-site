Introduction

Web browsers are one of the most important parts of the World Wide Web. They act as the main connection between users and the internet, allowing access to websites, applications, and online services. While they may seem simple, browsers are complex systems that interpret HTML, CSS, and JavaScript to create interactive experiences.

Understanding how browsers work is important for web developers because they directly affect performance, security, and user experience. This website explores the role, history, structure, and future of web browsers.
